# Community

This Ansible collection is powered by passionate contributors who bring real-world
expertise from the field. Every module, every feature, and every improvement comes from
professionals who are automating VMware Cloud Foundation at scale.

Whether you're filing a bug report, proposing a game-changing feature, submitting a 
documentation fix, or sharing your deployment story—**your contribution matters**.

## Support and Maintenance

:octicons-heart-24: &nbsp; This collection is crafted by members of the
**Broadcom Professional Services** community who live and breathe VMware Cloud
Foundation automation every day. We are practitioners, innovators, and fellow automation
enthusiasts.

This Ansible collection is **not** supported by Broadcom Support. It thrives on the
collaboration and input from the community and our customers to continuously improve.

**Translation?** We're here to help, we're passionate about what we build, and we
respond when we can; howver, but we can't promise 24/7 support or SLAs. What we *can*
promise is a community that genuinely cares about making this collection awesome to
enable you and your customers to do more with VMware Cloud Foundation and Ansible.

## Getting Help

If you need assistance:

:octicons-heart-24: &nbsp; Review the and examples provided in this repository.

:octicons-issue-opened-24: &nbsp; Use the GitHub [issues][issues] to report bugs or
suggest enhancements on behalf of the community and our customers.

:octicons-search-24: &nbsp; Before opening an issue, please [search the issues][issues-search]
and use the reactions to add votes to matching issues. Please include as much
information as you can. Details like these are incredibly useful in helping the us
evaluate and prioritize any changes:

- A reproducible test case or series of steps.
- Any modifications you've made relevant to the bug.
- Anything unusual about your environment or deployment.

:octicons-thumbsup-24: &nbsp; Issues are monitored by the maintainers and are
prioritized based on criticality and [reactions][reactions].

:octicons-comment-discussion-24: &nbsp; You can also start a discussion on the GitHub
[discussions][discussions] area to ask questions or share ideas.
